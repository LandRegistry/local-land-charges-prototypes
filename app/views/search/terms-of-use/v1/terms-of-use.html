About this service

Local land charges can restrict the way you use land and property. Use this service to check for local land charges in England and Wales.

Before you start, you should check the authority you want to search is on the register.

You can search for a local land charge by:

  • postcode or street name
  • map
  • title number
  • coordinates
  • INSPIRE ID
! Warning This service will not tell you if a local land charge is being added to the register while you search.

By using this service, you accept all the terms of use on this page. If a court decides any of the terms are unlawful, the rest will continue to be effective. The terms of use do not restrict your access to local land charges register information under the Environmental Information Regulations 2004 (EIR).

You can stop using this service at any time. HM Land Registry can stop you from using this service if you do not follow its terms of use.

Ways to search

Personal search

A personal search is free. It provides:

  • a description of the local land charge
  • the registration dates
  • contact details for the authority to answer questions about the charge (where available)
  • access to the local land charges register under the EIR

Official search

An official search service costs £7.

The official search will show the same information as the personal search plus:

  • search boundary or area on a map
  • originating authority name and reference
  • any electronic links to a document or register that is relevant to a charge
  • local land charge boundary

You can also download and print the official search certificate as a secure document. This is digitally signed by HM Land Registry.

After your first search, you can make free repeat ones on the same search area for 6 months. You will get an official search certificate for each search application.

Things to know when buying an official search certificate

By using the official search service, you agree to be bound by and pay for any official search certificate you apply for.

Because the official search certificate information is provided immediately, once you pay for it you lose the right of cancellation you would normally have under The Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.

This means that by applying to buy an official search on land or property you have identified you agree:

  • to pay the fee for this service by making an online transaction (through GOV.PAY)
  • to receive the local land charge official search certificate as soon as you have paid
  • to lose your right to cancel once the official search certificate is available to view or download

HM Land Registry is not responsible for the security or privacy of information you provide when making a transaction through the service’s online payment provider. If you have an issue with this, you should:

  • contact the online payment provider directly
  • tell HM Land Registry about the outcome by calling 0300 006 0411 or Welsh (Monday to Friday 9am to 5pm)

What is not included in the search service

Searches do not provide information on:

  • planning and development applications
  • building controls
  • road development schemes near to your property

All CON29 standard search information enquiries about issues that affect a property should be to the relevant authority.

Create an account

You need to create an account to access the search service.

To create an account you need to provide your:

  • name
  • email address
  • password

For 6 months your account will provide you with:

  • a search result archive
  • unlimited downloads
  • a dashboard showing active and expired searches
  • online storage for your searches
If you do not use your account for 12 months, we will delete it.

You are responsible for:

  • keeping your username and password secure
  • all activity that occurs on or through your account

If you think your account security is at risk, contact HM Land Registry:

  • phone: 0300 006 0411
  • speak to a Welsh speaker: 0300 006 0422

We are not responsible for any losses. For example, if someone uses your account details to sign in.

Your agreement with us

When you use the Search for local land charges service, you enter into an agreement with HM Land Registry. We agree to provide you with the facility to exercise your right to personally inspect the register or to apply for an official search certificate for the land or property selected by you at the time and date of your transaction.

You can end this agreement at any time before you make a payment (official search certificate). The agreement is between you and HM Land Registry and no-one else can enforce any of its terms.

The online payment provider will confirm your payment and provide a transaction reference.

Your agreement with HM Land Registry ends when you complete your personal inspection of the register or when your official search certificate is made available for you to view or download.

How we protect your information

HM Land Registry complies with the requirements set by the Data Protection Law.

We use your personal information in accordance with the HM Land Registry’s Personal Information Charter to:

  • create and manage any account you open
  • meet our agreement obligations by supplying the personal and official search results to you, once the online payment provider confirms that your payment has been processed and is complete (official search)
  • record the search
  • contact you using the email address you provide

We may keep and share information with:

  • other government departments
  • law enforcement and regulatory bodies, including specified anti-fraud organisations under section 68 Serious Crime Act 2007

This sharing of information helps to prevent and detect crime, for example, property fraud. It also helps with the investigation of land banking schemes.

We will:

  • keep your personal information secure
  • not make it available for commercial use, such as marketing, without your permission
  • share your information with other HM Land Registry teams to process your application

HM Land Registry is not liable to you or any other person for losses caused by:

  • delays to this service

For example, service delays from:

  • interruptions to the internet
  • services provided by third parties (anyone who is not HM Land Registry)
  • false information from linked websites

Copyright and database rights

The information in this service is subject to Crown copyright and database right. Also, use of address and mapping data (including the link between the address and its location) in this service is subject to Ordnance Survey licence terms and conditions (link opens in new tab).

The information in the local land charges register may also include information in which other third parties (including the authority who registered the charge) claim copyright and/or database right. Therefore, reuse of this information for a purpose other than conducting a search of the local land charges register would require specific consent.

Your information and how we use it

Make a complaint

If you are unhappy with the service, contact HM Land Registry. You can:

  • complete the online contact form
  • phone: 0300 006 0411
  • speak to a Welsh speaker: 0300 006 0422

If you are unhappy with the answer you receive, follow the HM Land Registry’s internal complaints procedure.

You can also contact the Independent Complaints Reviewer’s Office (ICR). They can investigate your complaint independently from HM Land Registry.
